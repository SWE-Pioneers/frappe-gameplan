# Moonhollow Studios — Demo Data World Bible

The universe behind Gameplan's demo data. Moonhollow Studios, its people, and
the game Common Room are fictional. Everything the demo content says must stay
consistent with this document.

---

## The studio

**Moonhollow Studios** — a 16-person, fully remote indie game studio, about two
years old. Formed by ex-colleagues who wanted to make small warm games instead
of big cold ones. No office; Gameplan is their office. Communication culture is
async-first: decisions happen in discussions, not calls. Time zones span San
Francisco to Tokyo, so "everyone's online" is never true — which is why threads,
not meetings, run the studio.

Tone of the forum overall: warm, competent, playful but not cutesy. People
disagree openly and kindly. Inside jokes exist (see cast) but are used sparingly.

## The game

**Common Room** — a cozy multiplayer hangout world. Pre-alpha.

> You and your friends share a clubhouse: a café counter, a library nook, a game
> room, a little conference hall for watch-parties. Furnish it, tend it, and let
> it become the place your group actually hangs out. No combat, no grind — the
> game is being somewhere together.

Made with pixel art (16×16 grid, rendered at 48px), top-down. Current world map
has four quadrants: **Café**, **Library**, **Game Room**, **Conference Hall**,
around a central **Common Room** lobby.

**Status & timeline hooks** (the narrative arcs are built on these):
- World map v2 recently planned and built (prefab/room pipeline)
- Café quadrant just furnished; collision bugs being ironed out
- First internal playtest happens mid-timeline
- Studio decides to aim for a **Steam Next Fest demo** near the end of the
  timeline — this announcement is the climax post
- A secret second-game prototype ("Project Bluebird") simmers in a small
  private community

## Narrative arcs (~4 weeks of virtual time, ending "today")

Week 1 — **World map v2.** Jonas posts the quadrant proposal; real debate
(Lena wants the library bigger; Priya worries about prefab scope). Decision
lands. Engineering posts the prefab/room pipeline plan. Poll: lobby rug.

Week 2 — **Furnishing & bugs.** Café quadrant furnished (renders posted, WIP
framing). Collision bug saga in QA (Dev's repro, Marcus's fix, collision-debug
screenshot). Zoe ships the in-house level editor. Sam
posts café ambience track. Hana runs a "remote rituals" People Ops thread.

Week 3 — **First internal playtest.** Announcement, then feedback threads:
what felt cozy, what felt empty; the game room wins, the conference hall
confuses people. Ingrid proposes environmental lore. Tasks spike. Felix ships
the door animation. Chloe shares early Discord-community reactions.

Week 4 — **Next Fest decision (climax).** Grace announces the Steam Next Fest
target. Marketing kicks off (Antoine: trailer-moment thread; poll on key art
direction from Tomás's options). Bluebird gets one intriguing pitch post and a
prototype-build note. Timeline ends with fresh, active, unread-for-Maya threads
so the demo opens on a living forum.

Background texture across all weeks: watercooler threads (what everyone's
playing, pet photos), a page or two per week (design docs, playtest checklist,
onboarding guide), small task churn, reactions everywhere.

## Custom emojis

The studio has its own emoji set (created by Chloe, in-fiction) — tiny badge-style
pixel graphics drawn from the game's world. Used in reactions alongside standard
emojis, weighted so they feel beloved, not forced:

- `:espresso:` — the too-tall espresso machine (the mascot; most-used reaction)
- `:cozy:` — the café armchair; means "this feels right"
- `:rug:` — the contested lobby rug; means "we're bikeshedding"
- `:doorclose:` — Felix's door animation; means "shipped!"
- `:bluebird:` — Project Bluebird; used only inside that community
- `:moonhollow:` — studio crescent-moon logo
- `:playtest:` — game controller; QA approval
- `:vinyl:` — Sam's vinyl-crackle crusade

Emoji images are original pixel art on transparent backgrounds (drawn for this
fixture, not taken from any asset pack).

## Content targets

- ~55 discussions, ~260 comments; length power-law (a few 15–25-comment
  threads, many 2–5, some 0)
- Reactions power-law; every persona reacts, few react everywhere; custom
  emojis make up roughly a quarter of reactions, `:espresso:` leading
- ~30 tasks across statuses (some assigned, 2–3 overdue), ~8 pages,
  3 polls with votes, 2 drafts (one is Maya's)
- Timestamps: personas post in their own timezone's working-ish hours;
  weekends near-silent except Marcus and watercooler
